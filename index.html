About Me

I am a PhD Candidate at the School of Photovoltaic and Renewable Energy Engineering, University of New South Wales (UNSW), Sydney. My research focuses on leveraging artificial intelligence and computational methods to accelerate the discovery and optimization of next-generation photovoltaic materials.

My work spans the intersection of machine learning, first-principles calculations, and experimental materials science. I am passionate about developing innovative approaches to address the global energy challenge through advanced solar cell technologies.

Research Interests

AI for Photovoltaics

Applying machine learning and artificial intelligence techniques to accelerate the discovery and optimization of next-generation photovoltaic materials.

First Principle Calculation

Utilizing VASP (Vienna Ab initio Simulation Package) for density functional theory calculations to understand material properties at the atomic level.

Quantum Dot Synthesis

Synthesizing and characterizing quantum dot materials for photovoltaic applications, exploring their unique optoelectronic properties.

Perovskite-Inspired Materials

Investigating perovskite and perovskite-inspired materials for high-efficiency, stable, and cost-effective solar energy conversion.
